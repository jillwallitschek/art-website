Jill Wallitschek

 

For some, the institutions of our upringing inform who we're supposed to be and who we're not supposed to be. With the best of intentions, we're taught what we should believe and how we should behave. It's binary, simplified, and uninclusive. Detached from our internal compass. A game with right or wrong. If we win, most of us also lose.

In practice, life could never be simplified so easily. Good comes with evil. We feel and we make mistakes. And regarless of whether we accept it, we continue to coexist with our contradictions.

I use art to both explore and honor my identity idependent of these expectations and sometimes in response to them. I'm interested in the dichotomy of our feelings, beliefs, and many of the societal constructs I've grown up with. I use art to express what I cannot always articulate through words. Such expression takes form through subject matter, structure, brush stroke, color, execution, and medium. I use art to document and honor my journey. My art is for me.
