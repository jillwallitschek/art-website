Growth & Expression

Jill R. Wallitschek

To live authentically you must be able to face every version of yourself. You must know and accept who you are, who you were, and who you want to be. Not who you were told to be. For each iteration, you must allow for the contradicting, simaltaneous, and ever evolving parts that make you whole, whether you feel that wholeness or not. We have to accept who we are and all of the layers that may encompass. I'm not always sure what I'm expressing for any given project. Symbolism can convey the most concrete of ideas, beliefs, and goals. For the rest, for everything else I need to articulate just to be, aware or not, that can be expressed through structure, brush stroke, color, and medium. For better or worse, that expression can relieve itself again and again until it is truly released.
