Expression

To live at all I had to meet every version of myself I chose to forget. I worked everyday to know and accept these versions for who they are, and not who they were told to be by the generations of supression before them. And for each iteration, I learned to coexist with the contradicting, simaltaneous, and ever evolving parts that make me whole.

I use art to explore the diachotomy of these feeling and beliefs. To express what is not traditionally acceptable to articulate through words. Such expression takes form through subject matter, structure, brush stroke, color, and medium.
