Jill Wallitschek

 

Throughout life, we're taught who we're supposed to be and who we're not supposed to be until we forget entirely who we really are. I think most of us never got a chance to meet that person in the first place - to know them intimately, for better and for worse.

It took me several years to even begin to understand that disconnect, let alone any institutions that created it. I knew I had lost something crucial yet my journey was for peace, not discovery. Along the way it became clear that to live at all I had to meet every version of myself I had chosen to forget.

I aim to know and fully accept these versions for who they are. For each iteration, I have learned to coexist with the contradicting, simaltaneous, and often erratic parts that make me human.

I use art to explore the diachotomy of these feeling, these beliefs, and these institutions. To express what is not traditionally acceptable to articulate through words. Such expression takes form through subject matter, structure, brush stroke, color, and medium. I use art to document and honor my journey.
